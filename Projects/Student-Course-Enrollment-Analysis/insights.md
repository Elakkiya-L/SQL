# 📊 Key Insights – Student Course Enrollment Analysis

## 🎯 Enrollment Patterns

* Student enrollments are unevenly distributed — some students take multiple courses while others are not enrolled at all.
* A small group of students shows high engagement by enrolling in several courses.

---

## 📚 Course Popularity

* Certain courses have significantly higher enrollments, indicating popularity or higher demand.
* Some courses have low or zero enrollments, which may require review or improvement.

---

## 👤 Student Engagement

* Students enrolled in more than 2 courses can be considered highly active learners.
* Students enrolled above the average number of courses represent the most engaged segment.

---

## ⚠️ Gaps Identified

* Presence of students not enrolled in any course highlights potential onboarding or engagement issues.
* Uneven distribution suggests that course selection is not balanced.

---

## 📈 Business/Academic Implications

* High-demand courses can be expanded or offered more frequently.
* Low-enrollment courses may need restructuring or better promotion.
* Non-enrolled students should be targeted for engagement programs.

---

## 🧠 Summary

* The dataset shows a mix of highly engaged students and inactive ones.
* Course demand varies significantly, offering opportunities for optimization.
